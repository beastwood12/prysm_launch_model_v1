How the Model Works

1. Prysm Adoption: Based on price sensitivity (lower price = higher adoption) and participation rate, with gradual rollout over time.

2. Direct Revenue: Prysm devices generate revenue through scans that convert to product orders (Scans √ó Conversion Rate √ó Average Order).

3. Engagement Effect: More scans per device create an engagement multiplier (up to 2x) that strengthens retention and subscription impacts.

4. Retention Improvements: Better retention means fewer sales leaders leave each month, with the effect scaling based on Prysm penetration and engagement. The 3-month retention improvement is applied as a monthly benefit (divided by 3).

5. Sales Leader Growth: Enhanced retention leads to more total sales leaders, while improved subscriptions drive new leader recruitment.

6. Business Impact: Three types of sales boosts apply based on penetration:
‚Ä¢ SCS Boost: Increased sales of SCS products (25% of total sales)
‚Ä¢ Subscription Boost: Enhanced subscription performance (strengthened by engagement)
‚Ä¢ Excitement Boost: General productivity increase across all sales categories
‚Ä¢ Cannibalization: Some SCS boost comes from existing sales rather than net new

7. Revenue from Additional Leaders: Each incremental new leader generates ~$1,171 in sales, each retained existing leader generates ~$2,972 in sales (regional averages from actual data).

8. Compounding Effects: All improvements compound monthly, with each month's enhanced metrics becoming the baseline for the next month's calculations.
